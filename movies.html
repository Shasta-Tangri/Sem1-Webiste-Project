Don't Look Up

What would you do if an asteroid was heading for earth and full destruction was imminent? Streaming on Netflix December 24th, Leonardo DiCaprio and Jennifer Lawrence star in Don’t Look Up as two low-level astronomers who attempt to warn mankind about their enviable destruction via a media tour. Directed, written and, produced by Adam McKay – DiCaprio and Lawrence are joined by a fully star-studded cast including Meryl Streep, Jonah Hill, Tyler Perry, and Ariana Grande, and much more in this Netflix Holiday release.

Mark your calendars for the end of the world! Or December 24th, whichever comes first.

Lightyear

Chris Evans hangs up his Captain America shield and dons a space suit in Pixar’s upcoming computer animated film Lightyear. The movie, set to be released to theaters on June 17th, 2022, explores the origin story of Buzz Lightyear, the inter galactic hero who was first seen in toy form in Pixar’s 1995 film Toy Story. Not much is known at this time regarding the plot of the film, but there is little doubt that this origin story will be sending us to infinity and beyond!

Be sure to watch out for any famous Pixar easter eggs when you head to the theaters to see Lightyear next year.

West Side Story

Stephen Spielberg’s West Side Story hit theaters on December 10th. West Side Story is a beloved musical that first hit in the Broadway stage in 1957. Winning 6 Tony’s in it’s original run, the popularity of the show inspired a switch to the silver screen in the 1961 film adaptation, winning 11 Academy Awards. Now 60 years later, Stephen Spielberg is bringing the story back to a modern audience.

Starring Ansel Elgort as Tony, and Rachel Zegler as Maria, this Romeo and Juliette inspired tale of star-crosses lovers has a little bit for everyone – singing, dancing, gang violence, and a romance for the ages.
