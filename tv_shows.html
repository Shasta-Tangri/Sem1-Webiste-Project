The Classics

What makes a classic a classic, anyway? What’s classic to me, at 25, is different than what my grandmother would call a classic. So consider this classics through the eyes of a millennial.

Friends. I think we can all agree that Friends is a classic. So why didn’t I watch it until 3 years ago? 6 white 30 somethings trying to make it in the big city. Okay Big Bang Theory, I hear you. Does that mean in 20 years we’ll call BBT a classic? Yikes. There are 10 seasons of Friends, having aired it’s first episode back in 1994. The series follows Monica, a high strung chef, her brother Ross, a paleontologist with a lesbian ex-wife, and their friends Chandler, a neurotic salesman, Joey, an out-of-work actor, Phoebe, a loopy guitarist, and Rachel, a serial romantic.

New & Notable

I think we can all agree that we’re living in the golden age of television. Here’s my top 5 bingeworthy list for this winter season.

5. Shameless

This show is aptly named; they truly have no shame. If the holidays bring up memories of crazy family members and catastrophic events in the past, find comfort in Shameless knowing that someone else’s family is definitely worse than yours.

4. Schitt's Creek

Are you starting to see a theme here? Another show about a family who has it a lot worse than you do, but you get to laugh and cry alongside them as they navigate rebuilding their lives in Schitt’s Creek.

3. Selling Sunset

Do you ever just feel mad? For no specific reason, just angry. Watch this show; now you have a reason.

2. Ginny & Georgia

Annnnd we’re back on brand here with Ginny & Georgia and their own special brand of dysfunction. With a killer soundtrack, this one will definitely take you for a ride.

1. Good Girls

The number one show to watch this winter is Good Girls. With 4 seasons to keep you busy while the weather outside is frightful, you’ll find warmth in these girl’s antics.
